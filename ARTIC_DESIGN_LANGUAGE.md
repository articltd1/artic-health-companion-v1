# ARTIC Design Language (ADL)

## Introduction
The ARTIC Design Language (ADL) is the official design standard for every ARTIC product and brand experience. ADL defines the visual, interaction, and experiential foundation for:
- ARTIC Health Companion
- ARTIC Hospital
- ARTIC Doctor Portal
- ARTIC AI
- ARTIC Research
- ARTIC Telemedicine
- ARTIC IoT
- ARTIC Robotics

ADL is built to support products that are professional, trustworthy, medical, AI-powered, minimal, premium, accessible, fast, elegant, and future-ready.

---

## 1. Design Philosophy
ARTIC design is built on a clear set of beliefs:
- Health technology should feel calm, dependable, and clinically intelligent.
- Simplicity enables confidence in complex workflows.
- AI should feel supportive, transparent, and human-centered.
- Premium design is created through clarity, spacing, and thoughtful detail.
- Every interface should be efficient, accessible, and built for enterprise-scale operations.

### Core values
- Professional: clinical clarity and precision.
- Trustworthy: transparent interaction and ethical data treatment.
- Medical: visual hygiene, measured information, and clinical tone.
- AI Powered: intelligent suggestions without overwhelming the user.
- Minimal: remove distraction, prioritize task flow.
- Premium: refined surfaces, typography, and motion.
- Accessible: inclusive for all users, including mobility, vision, and cognitive differences.
- Fast: efficient patterns, instant feedback, and predictable layouts.
- Future ready: modular systems that adapt across domains and devices.

## 3. Visual Identity
ADL uses restrained visual systems that balance medical authority with modern digital elegance.

### Brand elements
- Soft glass surfaces
- Rounded corners
- Purposeful whitespace
- Subtle gradients for depth
- Consistent iconography
- Clear hierarchy in typography and spacing

### Logo usage
- Use the ARTIC logo consistently across product signage and onboarding.
- Primary lockup should appear in top-left header, login screens, and core brand pages.
- Secondary mark may be used in compact spaces, badges, and app icons.
- Maintain generous clearspace around the logo (at least 1x the height of the logomark).
- Avoid heavy textures or busy backgrounds behind the mark.
- Use monochrome white or dark versions on high contrast surfaces.

## 5. Typography
A refined type system supports readability, hierarchy, and accessible information.

### Primary fonts
- `Inter` — for body text and interface readability.
- `Plus Jakarta Sans` — for headings and premium expression.

### Font scale
- `Display / Hero`: 4rem
- `Heading 1`: 2.5rem
- `Heading 2`: 2rem
- `Heading 3`: 1.5rem
- `Heading 4`: 1.25rem
- `Body Large`: 1.125rem
- `Body`: 1rem
- `Small`: 0.875rem

### Weights
- `Extra Bold` / `Bold` — for headings, CTA labels, and strong emphasis.
- `Medium` — for subheads, summaries, and button text.
- `Regular` — for body copy and long-form text.
- `Light` — sparingly for large display copy or visual accents.

### Typographic hierarchy
- Use generous line-height for readability: 1.5 for body, 1.3 for headings.
- Keep titles and metadata visually distinct through size, weight, and color.
- Limit long lines to ~65-75 characters for body copy.

---

## 6. Spacing Scale
ADL uses a consistent modular spacing system to create rhythm and clarity.

### Scale
- `xs`: 0.25rem / 4px
- `sm`: 0.5rem / 8px
- `md`: 1rem / 16px
- `lg`: 1.5rem / 24px
- `xl`: 2rem / 32px
- `2xl`: 3rem / 48px
- `3xl`: 4rem / 64px

### Principles
- Use multiples of 8px for layout spacing.
- Provide generous padding inside cards and panels.
- Use larger spacing between major sections.
- Reserve tighter spacing for compact data dense interfaces only.

---

## 7. Grid System
A 12-column responsive grid supports modular layouts across products.

### Breakpoints
- `xs`: 0–639px
- `sm`: 640–767px
- `md`: 768–1023px
- `lg`: 1024–1279px
- `xl`: 1280–1535px
- `2xl`: 1536px+

### Grid rules
- Standard gutter: 24px on desktop, 16px on tablet, 12px on mobile.
- Use 12-column layout for dashboards, forms, and data pages.
- On mobile, stack cards vertically with full-width sections.
- Use consistent card and panel widths for visual balance.

---

## 8. Iconography
Lucide-style linear icons are the standard for ARTIC experiences.

### Icon principles
- Line-based, geometric, and simple.
- Consistent stroke width across applications.
- Use rounded terminals for approachable medical tone.
- Avoid overly decorative or detailed icons.
- Maintain clear meaning with minimal visual noise.

### Usage
- Navigation: simple, recognizable glyphs.
- Status: colored badges only when paired with text.
- Actions: icon + label combination for clarity.
- System icons should be used at consistent sizes: 16px, 20px, 24px.

---

## 9. Illustration Style
Illustrations should feel modern, clinical, and subtly futuristic.

### Style guidelines
- Use soft gradients and minimal line art.
- Favor schematic medical scenes over literal photography.
- Keep character and device illustrations friendly but professional.
- Use color sparingly with brand palette accents.
- Prefer abstract, clinical metaphors for AI, data, and connection.

### Use cases
- Landing hero illustrations.
- Empty states and onboarding visuals.
- High-level conceptual sections for AI, Telemedicine, Robotics.

---

## 10. Photography Style
Photography should support the product’s trustworthy, premium healthcare identity.

### Principles
- Natural clinical environments with professional clinicians and patients.
- Soft, even lighting and clean, minimal backgrounds.
- Authentic, diverse representation in medical settings.
- Use imagery that reinforces care, collaboration, and technology.
- Avoid overly staged or dramatic medical scenarios.

---

## 11. Cards
Cards are the core surface for content organization.

### Card types
- `Surface Card`: basic content container.
- `Metric Card`: highlight key KPI values.
- `Summary Card`: quick overview with icon and action.
- `Action Card`: invites user interaction.
- `Table Card`: houses data tables with header actions.

### Card style
- Rounded corners: 24px radius.
- Soft shadows for elevation.
- Subtle border and background separation.
- Clear title, body, and footer structure.
- Use glassmorphism for premium panels when a strong visual hierarchy is needed.

---

## 12. Buttons
Buttons are strong, accessible, and purposeful.

### Variants
- `Primary`: filled brand blue, for main actions.
- `Secondary`: soft outline or secondary fill for supportive actions.
- `Ghost`: low emphasis actions.
- `Danger`: destructive actions.
- `Icon`: compact circular actions.

### Style
- Rounded shape with 16px radius.
- Min height: 44px.
- Clear focus ring for keyboard users.
- Use text labels with optional icons.
- Disabled state uses lower opacity and muted border.

---

## 13. Forms
Forms must be clear, efficient, and accessible.

### Field types
- Text input
- Select / dropdown
- Multi-select chips
- Radio group
- Checkbox
- Textarea
- Date/time picker
- Toggle switch

### Form principles
- Label above the field.
- Helper text under the field.
- Error text in red with icon.
- Group related fields with section headings.
- Use full-width inputs in mobile layouts.

### Validation
- Show inline validation messages.
- Avoid red-only messaging; include icons and concise text.
- Provide clear success or error states after submission.

---

## 14. Tables
Tables display structured clinical and operational data.

### Table design
- Clean header row with strong labels.
- Zebra row alternation for readability.
- Sticky header on scrollable tables.
- Column actions aligned to the right.
- Responsive row expansion for mobile.

### Interaction
- Sortable columns when data is sortable.
- Row actions exposed as icon buttons or menu.
- Status pills for appointment, lab, and prescription states.
- Accessible table markup with proper headings.

---

## 15. Charts
Charts communicate trend-based and comparative data clearly.

### Chart types
- Line chart
- Bar chart
- Area chart
- Donut chart
- Sparkline
- KPI trend card

### Chart principles
- Minimal axes and labels.
- Use brand and semantic colors consistently.
- Highlight key values with callout labels.
- Provide alternative text and data tables for accessibility.
- Keep charts lightweight and easy to scan.

---

## 16. Dialogs
Dialogs should feel focused and non-disruptive.

### Dialog style
- Centered or slide-over panels.
- Clear title, body, and action footer.
- Strong visual separation from background.
- Backdrop with subtle opacity.
- Close button and escape support.

### Use cases
- Confirmations
- Form entry
- Details preview
- Alerts and warnings

---

## 17. Notifications
Notification systems keep users informed without distraction.

### Types
- Toast: transient success/error feedback.
- Banner: in-page system status or alerts.
- Inbox/center: persisted notifications.

### Style
- Clear iconography and concise text.
- Strong color coding for `success`, `warning`, `danger`, `info`.
- Action buttons when needed.
- Dismissible, with optional snooze.

---

## 18. Navigation
Navigation must be intuitive and role-aware.

### Sidebar
- Primary navigation area for enterprise workflows.
- Collapsible on desktop.
- Icons + labels for clarity.
- Group items by feature domain.
- Keep the active state visible and consistent.

### Topbar
- Global search.
- Notifications.
- Role indicator / app switcher.
- User profile and quick actions.
- Lightweight breadcrumbs when deep in workflow.

### Footer
- Minimal footer for product apps.
- Use for legal links, version, and support information.
- Keep footer subtle and unobtrusive.

---

## 19. Animations
Motion enhances clarity and system personality.

### Transition palette
- `fast`: 120ms ease-out
- `standard`: 220ms ease-out
- `smooth`: 320ms ease-out

### Use cases
- Page transitions: subtle fade and slide.
- Hover states: gentle lift and shadow expansion.
- Accordions/tabs: smooth height and opacity changes.
- Toasts: slide in/out with spring.

### Motion principles
- Support `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Use motion to clarify state changes, not decorate.
- Keep animation durations short and purposeful.
- Avoid over-animation in data-dense screens.

---

## 20. Dark Mode
Dark mode is a first-class ARTIC experience.

### Dark theme tone
- Deep charcoal backgrounds.
- Soft luminous accents.
- High contrast text on dark surfaces.
- Muted surface cards with subtle borders.

### Dark mode rules
- Preserve brand accents.
- Use light outlines and glow sparingly.
- Maintain readability and color contrast.
- Avoid pure black surfaces where possible; use dark navy/charcoal.

---

## 21. Accessibility
Accessibility is non-negotiable across ARTIC products.

### Key standards
- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.
- Keyboard navigable interfaces.
- Screen reader-friendly semantics.
- Visible focus states.
- Color contrast minimum 4.5:1 for text.
- Support for reduced motion.
- Accessible forms and labels.

### Healthcare accessibility
- Large touch targets for clinical environments.
- Clear error state guidance.
- Support assistive technologies in fast-paced workflows.
- Prioritize readability for older and visually impaired users.

---

## 22. Healthcare Design Principles
Design for real patient care and clinical workflow.

### Principles
- Clinical clarity: present information with precision.
- Decision support: surface the right insight at the right time.
- Safety first: prevent errors with clear affordances.
- Privacy and trust: design for confidentiality and consent.
- Calm urgency: use visual hierarchy to distinguish routine from critical.

### Experience goals
- Patients should feel supported and informed.
- Clinicians should feel empowered, not overwhelmed.
- Administrators should see operational clarity.

---

## 23. AI Interaction Guidelines
AI should feel intelligent, explainable, and user-centered.

### AI design rules
- Present AI suggestions as recommendations, not directives.
- Always surface reasoning or confidence where appropriate.
- Keep AI interfaces straightforward and actionable.
- Use microcopy to explain what the AI did.
- Avoid over-automation; keep final control with the user.

### AI patterns
- `Insight cards` with quick explanations.
- `Suggested actions` as pill buttons.
- `Confidence badges` for model certainty.
- `Learn more` links for clinical reasoning.

---

## 24. Voice UI Guidelines
Prepare for future voice-enabled interactions.

### Voice design principles
- Use simple command language.
- Keep voice responses short, clear, and polite.
- Provide typed alternatives for all voice actions.
- Confirm critical actions before execution.
- Respect privacy and ambient awareness.

### Voice UI use cases
- Quick appointment status queries.
- Health summary recaps.
- Hands-free clinician workflow support.
- Action confirmation in sterile or mobile environments.

---

## 25. Future Robotics Dashboard Guidelines
Robotics UI must combine operational precision with mission clarity.

### Robotics visual rules
- Use a dark, high-contrast control palette.
- Surface telemetry and system health prominently.
- Use minimal data panels with clear status states.
- Include real-time alerts, sensors, and task progress.
- Support remote monitoring with both summary and detail modes.

### Robotics experience goals
- Operators should understand system state instantly.
- Critical failures must be visually prioritized.
- Autonomous actions should be explainable.
- Control elements should be clearly separated from status displays.

---

## 26. Implementation Guidance
ARTIC Design Language is a living standard.

### Adoption
- Use ADL as the source of truth for UI design and product decisions.
- Apply ADL tokens across design files, code, and component libraries.
- Align product teams on brand language and interaction patterns.

### Governance
- Maintain ADL updates through a dedicated design review process.
- Version the design language as new products and capabilities emerge.
- Preserve consistency while allowing domain-specific variation.

### Future proofing
- Keep tokenized values for colors, spacing, typography, and motion.
- Build components that can be reused across ARTIC Health Companion, Hospital, Doctor Portal, AI, Research, Telemedicine, IoT, and Robotics.
- Design for expansion to voice, robotics, and advanced AI modalities.
